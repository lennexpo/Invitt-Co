How to Build a Website for Your Small Business in Zimbabwe

By Invitt Co  ·  March 2026  ·  6 min read
← Back to Blog

Building a website for your small business in Zimbabwe doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. What it does require is a clear plan, the right tools, and an understanding of what makes a website actually work for your business — not just look good.

This guide walks you through every step, from choosing a domain name to going live, with practical advice tailored specifically for the Zimbabwean market.

Step by Step: Building Your Business Website

01

Choose a Domain Name

Your domain is your website address — e.g. yourbusiness.co.zw. For Zimbabwe businesses, a .co.zw domain is ideal as it signals local credibility to both Google and your customers. Keep it short, memorable, and matching your business name. Register it through a local registrar like WebZim or ZISPA.

02

Choose a Hosting Platform

Hosting is where your website files live. For small businesses in Zimbabwe, GitHub Pages is a great free option for static websites. For more complex sites, affordable hosting providers like Netlify or local shared hosting work well. The key is choosing something reliable with good uptime.

03

Plan Your Pages

A solid small business website needs at minimum: a Home page, About page, Services page, and Contact page. Think about what your customer needs to know before they pick up the phone or send an enquiry. Every page should answer a question they have.

04

Design for Mobile First

Over 80% of Zimbabweans browse the internet on their phones. Your website must look and work perfectly on a small screen. If a visitor has to pinch and zoom to read your content, they will leave. Mobile-first design is not optional — it's essential.

05

Optimise for Google

A beautiful website that nobody finds is useless. Basic SEO — the right page titles, meta descriptions, and keywords — helps Google understand what your business does and show it to the right people. Include your location naturally in your text, e.g. "web design agency in Harare" or "plumber in Bulawayo."

06

Add a Contact Form and WhatsApp Button

Make it as easy as possible for customers to reach you. A contact form, a visible phone number, and a WhatsApp button are essential for Zimbabwe businesses where WhatsApp is the primary communication tool. Every enquiry you miss is revenue lost.

07

Connect Google Analytics and Search Console

Once your site is live, connect Google Analytics to track visitors and Google Search Console to monitor how you're performing in search results. Both are free and give you invaluable data for growing your online presence over time.

Should You Build It Yourself or Hire Someone?

Website builders like Wix or WordPress make it possible to build a basic site yourself. However, a DIY website often looks generic, loads slowly, and isn't properly optimised for Google — meaning you get a site but not the customers.
