Google Business Profile vs Website — Do You Need Both for Your Zimbabwe Business?

By Lennon Nyamajiwa April 2026 6 min read

Many business owners in Zimbabwe ask this question when they are deciding where to spend limited time and money online: "Do I need both a Google Business Profile and a website? Can I just use one?" The answer is that both serve fundamentally different purposes — and for most businesses in Zimbabwe, you need both. Here is why.

What a Google Business Profile Does

Your Google Business Profile controls how your business appears on Google Maps and in the local results that appear at the top of Google searches for location-specific queries. It shows your business name, address, phone number, opening hours, photos, reviews, and a link to your website. It is how local customers in Harare find you when they search for a service "near me" or in a specific area.

It is powerful, it is free, and it is essential for any business with a local customer base. But it has significant limitations.

What a Google Business Profile Cannot Do

  • It cannot tell the full story of your business — a listing gives you a description, photos, and reviews, but it cannot explain your process, showcase a detailed portfolio, or build a brand experience
  • It cannot rank for competitive non-local keywords — it only helps with local map-based searches, not general informational searches where your website can rank
  • It does not give you a professional address — every quote you send, every email you write, every business card you hand out needs a website, not a Google listing URL
  • It is entirely under Google's control — Google can change its display, suspend your listing, or alter the format at any time. You do not own the space.

What a Website Does That a Google Business Profile Cannot

Your website is digital property that you own. It can be as detailed as your business requires, optimised for any keyword you want to rank for, and built to convert visitors into enquiries through strategic design and copywriting. It is your 24/7 salesperson — available to anyone, anywhere, at any time.

A professional website also signals credibility in a way that a Google listing alone does not. When a potential customer in Zimbabwe is comparing businesses and one has a polished, professional website while the other only has a Google listing — the one with the website wins the trust battle almost every time.

How They Work Together

The most effective setup is both working in tandem. Your Google Business Profile drives local discovery — it puts you on the map and generates calls and direction requests from people who are ready to act. Your website then converts the more considered buyers — the people who found you on Google, clicked through to learn more, read your services page, looked at your portfolio, and decided to get in touch.

Google also ranks businesses with complete, active profiles — including a linked website — higher in local results. So having a strong website actually improves your Google Maps ranking as well.

The short answer: Start with your Google Business Profile because it is free and fast. But build your website as soon as your budget allows — because it is the only part of your online presence that you truly own and control.

What About Just Using Facebook or Instagram?

Social media pages and Google Business Profiles are both rented space — you do not own them, and the platform can change the rules at any time.
