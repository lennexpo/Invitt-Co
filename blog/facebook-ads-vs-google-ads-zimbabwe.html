Facebook Ads vs Google Ads — Which Works Better for Zimbabwe Businesses?

By Lennon Nyamajiwa April 2026 7 min read

If you have a budget to spend on digital advertising for your Zimbabwe business and you are trying to decide between Facebook Ads and Google Ads — you are asking the right question. These two platforms dominate digital advertising, and they work in fundamentally different ways. The right choice for your business depends entirely on what you are selling and who you are selling it to. Here is a clear breakdown.

The Core Difference: Intent vs Discovery

This is the most important thing to understand about both platforms. Google Ads targets intent — it shows your ad to people who are actively searching for what you offer right now. Facebook Ads targets discovery — it shows your ad to people who match a certain profile, even if they are not currently looking for your product.

Neither is better in absolute terms. But for different types of businesses and different goals, one will consistently outperform the other.

When Google Ads Wins for Zimbabwe Businesses

Google Ads is most powerful for businesses where customers actively search for the service before buying. If someone needs a plumber, an electrician, a driving school, a borehole drilled, a wedding venue, or a web designer — they go to Google. They have high intent. They are ready to act. A Google Ad placed at the top of those results, with a compelling offer and a well-designed landing page, can generate direct enquiries at a very reasonable cost.

Google Ads works particularly well for service businesses in Harare because the local competition for many keywords is still relatively low — meaning the cost per click is affordable compared to markets like South Africa or the UK.

When Facebook Ads Wins for Zimbabwe Businesses

Facebook Ads excels when you need to create awareness or desire for something that people do not spontaneously search for. If you are launching a new food product, promoting an event, running a clothing brand, or selling something that requires visual appeal and storytelling to sell — Facebook is your platform. You can precisely target by location (Harare, Bulawayo, etc.), age, interests, and behaviours, and use images and video to create desire before someone even knows they want what you are selling.

Facebook is also effective for retargeting — showing ads to people who have already visited your website, keeping your brand top-of-mind until they are ready to buy.

The Cost Reality for Zimbabwe

Facebook Ads generally have a lower entry point — you can run a campaign for $5–$10 per day and see results. Google Ads can be more expensive per click in competitive categories, but the quality of the leads is typically higher because you are reaching people who are already in buying mode.

A common mistake for Zimbabwe businesses is running Facebook Ads as their only strategy because the entry cost is lower — while missing out on Google Ads where the highest-intent customers are actively searching for them right now.

The Honest Recommendation

For most service businesses in Harare: start with Google Ads. You are putting your business in front of people who already want what you offer. Once your Google Ads are generating consistent returns, add Facebook Ads to expand your reach and build brand awareness.

For product businesses, e-commerce, or events: Facebook first, then Google once you know which products perform best.
