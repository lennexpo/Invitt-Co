How Much Does a Website Cost in Zimbabwe in 2026?

By Lennon Nyamajiwa April 2026 8 min read

If you have ever searched how much does a website cost in Zimbabwe, you have probably found vague answers, outdated prices, or agencies quoting in South African rand that make no sense for your business. This article fixes that. We are going to break down exactly what websites cost in Zimbabwe in 2026, what drives those prices up or down, and how to make sure you spend your money wisely.

Why Website Pricing in Zimbabwe Varies So Much

The range is enormous. You can find someone on Facebook offering a website for $50, and you can find agencies charging $5,000 or more. Both exist. Neither is automatically wrong for every situation. The difference comes down to what you are actually getting — and that is where most business owners get confused or end up with regret.

Three things determine what a website costs in Zimbabwe: who builds it, what it needs to do, and how much ongoing support is included.

Website Cost Tiers in Zimbabwe — What You Actually Get

Tier 1: $50 – $200 (Freelancer, Template-Based)

At this price point you are getting a basic template installed on WordPress or Wix with your logo and some text swapped in. It looks like a website. It probably loads. But it will not be optimised for Google, will not convert visitors into customers, and will almost certainly need to be rebuilt within 18 months as your business grows. Fine for a very early-stage business testing the waters. Not fine if you are trying to compete.

Tier 2: $350 – $750 (Small Agency / Professional Freelancer)

This is the most important tier for Zimbabwean SMEs. At this level you should be getting a properly designed, mobile-responsive website built with SEO fundamentals in place, fast load times, a working contact form, and copy that is structured to convert visitors. At Invitt Co, our Starter package ($350) and Growth package ($750) sit here — and they include everything a small business in Harare needs to compete online.

Tier 3: $1,000 – $3,000+ (Custom / E-commerce / Complex)

If you need an online store, a booking system, a client portal, or a highly custom design, this is where you are headed. This tier requires significantly more development time and ongoing maintenance. For most Harare SMEs, this is not necessary at the start — but it becomes relevant as you scale.

What Should Always Be Included — Regardless of Price

  • Mobile responsiveness (over 70% of Zimbabwean users browse on mobile)
  • Basic on-page SEO (title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure)
  • Fast load time (under 3 seconds)
  • A working contact form or WhatsApp button
  • SSL certificate (the padlock in the browser — essential for trust)
  • At least 30 days of post-launch support

If a quote does not include these, walk away or negotiate hard.

Quick tip: Always ask any web designer in Zimbabwe to show you live examples of websites they have built. If they cannot, that tells you everything you need to know.

Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

The upfront design fee is rarely the full picture. Make sure you ask about domain registration (typically $15–$25/year for a .co.zw), hosting ($50–$200/year depending on the provider), and any ongoing maintenance fees. Some agencies in Zimbabwe charge separately for every small update — find out before you sign.

Is a Cheap Website Worth It?

A $50 website that drives no traffic, loads slowly, and looks unprofessional costs you far more than $50. Every potential customer who lands on it and leaves immediately is a lost sale. Think of your website as a salesperson that works 24/7 — you get what you pay for.

At the same time, you do not need to spend $3,000 to have an effective online presence in Zimbabwe. A professionally built $350–$750 website, done properly, will outperform a $2,000 template site every time.
