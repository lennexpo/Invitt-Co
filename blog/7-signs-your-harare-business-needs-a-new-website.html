7 Signs Your Harare Business Needs a New Website

By Lennon Nyamajiwa April 2026 6 min read

Your website might look fine to you — after all, you see it every day. But the question is not whether it looks fine to you. The question is whether it is working hard enough to bring in new customers and represent your business professionally to people who have never heard of you. Here are seven clear signs that your current website is holding your Harare business back.

1. It Does Not Work Properly on Mobile

Over 70% of internet users in Zimbabwe browse on their phones. If your website looks squished, requires horizontal scrolling, or has buttons that are impossible to tap on a mobile screen — you are losing the majority of your visitors before they even read a word. Open your site on your phone right now. If anything feels off, that is a critical problem that needs fixing immediately.

2. It Takes More Than 3 Seconds to Load

Research shows that 53% of mobile users abandon a website that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. In Zimbabwe, where many users are on mobile data, speed is even more critical. A slow website does not just frustrate visitors — it also ranks lower on Google. If your site takes 5, 6, or more seconds to load, it is actively costing you business.

3. You Cannot Update It Yourself

If changing a price, adding a new service, or updating your phone number requires you to call your web designer and wait days for a response — your website is not working for you. A modern website should have a simple content management system that lets you (or a member of your team) make basic updates without any technical knowledge.

4. It Has Not Generated a Single Enquiry in the Last 6 Months

A website that generates zero enquiries is not a website — it is a digital brochure that nobody reads. If your site has a contact form, track whether anyone is using it. If nobody has submitted an enquiry in months, something is fundamentally wrong: either nobody is finding your site, or those who do find it are not compelled to contact you. Either way, it needs fixing.

5. It Does Not Show Up on Google

Search for your main service plus "Harare" — for example, "accountant Harare" or "cleaning service Zimbabwe." If your website does not appear on page one, or does not appear at all, your SEO is broken. A website that cannot be found is like a shop in the middle of nowhere with no signage. Read more: Why Your Business Isn't Showing Up on Google.

6. It Looks Significantly Worse Than Your Competitors

Perception matters enormously in business. If a potential customer in Harare is comparing you to a competitor and your website looks dated, unprofessional, or cluttered compared to theirs — they will choose the competitor. Your website is often the first impression someone has of your business. Make sure it creates the right one.

7. It Was Built More Than 3–4 Years Ago

Web design standards, SEO best practices, and user expectations all evolve. A website built in 2020 or earlier is likely missing features and optimisations that are now considered standard — structured data, Core Web Vitals optimisation, modern accessibility features, and updated security protocols. At a minimum, a website more than four years old deserves a thorough technical audit.

The bottom line: Your website should be your best salesperson — working 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, representing your business to everyone who searches for what you offer. If it is not doing that job, it needs to change.
