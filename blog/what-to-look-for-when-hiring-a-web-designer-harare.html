What to Look for When Hiring a Web Designer in Harare

By Lennon Nyamajiwa April 2026 7 min read

Hiring a web designer in Harare is not as simple as finding someone who knows how to use WordPress. The difference between a great web designer and a mediocre one is not just visual — it is the difference between a website that brings in customers and one that just sits there looking pretty. Before you pay anyone a single dollar, here is exactly what to look for.

1. Ask to See Live, Working Websites They Have Built

This is non-negotiable. Any web designer worth hiring in Zimbabwe should be able to show you multiple live websites they have built for real clients. Not mockups. Not screenshots. Actual URLs you can visit, test on your phone, and evaluate. Click through the sites. Check how fast they load. See if they look professional on mobile. If a designer cannot show you live work, move on.

Test this: When they share portfolio links, open each one on your mobile phone. If it looks broken, squished, or unprofessional on mobile — that designer is not good enough for your business.

2. Ask About Their SEO Knowledge

A beautiful website that nobody finds is a wasted investment. Your web designer does not need to be an SEO expert, but they absolutely must understand the basics: proper heading structure (H1, H2, H3), title tags and meta descriptions, image alt text, fast load times, and mobile responsiveness. Ask them directly: "What SEO practices do you include as standard in every website you build?" If they look confused or give you a vague answer, that tells you everything.

3. Understand What Is Included in the Price

Web design quotes in Zimbabwe can be deceptively simple on the surface. Always ask what is specifically included. Will you own the domain? Is hosting included? For how long? What happens if you need changes after launch — are edits charged separately? Is there a maintenance fee? Get every detail in writing before you agree to anything.

4. Ask About Their Process and Timeline

A professional web designer has a defined process: discovery, design, content, development, review, launch. They should be able to tell you how long each stage takes and give you a realistic launch date. Be very wary of designers who promise a "website in 24 hours" — unless it is a genuinely simple one-page site, this typically means a template with your logo slapped on it, nothing more.

At Invitt Co, we guarantee your website goes live within 14 days — or you do not pay until it does. That commitment forces us to be organised and on schedule.

5. Check Whether They Write Copy or Expect You To

Many web designers in Harare will build your site but expect you to provide all the text content. This is a problem for most business owners who are not writers. Ask upfront whether copywriting is included or whether it is extra. Good web copy — the words on your website — is arguably more important than the design itself when it comes to converting visitors into customers.

6. Assess Their Communication Style

A web project involves ongoing communication — feedback rounds, revisions, questions about your business, content approvals. A designer who takes two days to reply to a WhatsApp message during the sales process will take a week to respond when you have a problem post-launch. Pay attention to how quickly and clearly they communicate from the very first conversation.

7. Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • No portfolio or only showing static mockups
  • Cannot explain what SEO is or why it matters
  • Asks for 100% payment upfront before starting
  • No written agreement or proposal
  • Cannot tell you who will own the domain and hosting after launch
  • Disappears after launching your site with no handover or support period

What a Great Web Designer Looks Like

The right web designer for your Harare business will ask questions about your customers, your goals, and your competitors before they ever open a design tool. They will guide you through the process, be honest about what you need versus what is nice-to-have, and deliver something that actually works for your business — not just something that looks good in a screenshot.
