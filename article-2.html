5 Digital Marketing Mistakes Zimbabwean Businesses Make

By Invitt Co  ·  March 2026  ·  6 min read
← Back to Blog

Most businesses in Zimbabwe are losing customers online without even realising it. The good news is that the most common digital marketing mistakes are easy to fix once you know what to look for. Here are the five we see most often — and exactly what to do about them.

01

Relying Only on Facebook or WhatsApp

Facebook and WhatsApp are excellent tools for staying in touch with existing customers. But they are not search engines. When someone in Harare searches Google for "printer repair near me" or "web design Harare", your Facebook page will not appear.

You need a presence on the platforms where people are actively searching for what you offer — and that means Google.

The Fix: Build a professional website optimised for Google search so new customers can find you when they are actively looking for your services.
02

Not Having a Google Business Profile

Google Business Profile is a free tool that puts your business on Google Maps and in local search results. When someone searches "office supplies Harare" or "web designer near me", businesses with a Google Business Profile appear prominently — often before any website results.

Thousands of Harare businesses are missing this completely free visibility opportunity every single day.

The Fix: Claim and complete your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. Add photos, your hours, phone number, and respond to reviews regularly.
03

Having a Website That Isn't Mobile Friendly

Over 80% of internet users in Zimbabwe browse on their phones. If your website doesn't work properly on a mobile device — small text, broken layouts, slow loading — visitors will leave within seconds and go straight to a competitor.

A website that looks great on a desktop but is broken on mobile is worse than having no website at all. It actively damages your credibility.

The Fix: Make sure your website is built with a mobile-first approach. Test it on your own phone. If it's hard to use, it needs to be rebuilt.
04

Posting Inconsistently on Social Media

Many businesses post enthusiastically for a week or two and then go silent for months. This tells potential customers that the business may not be active or reliable. Consistency builds trust and keeps you top of mind.

It's far better to post twice a week consistently than to post every day for two weeks and then disappear.

The Fix: Create a simple content calendar. Even 2-3 posts per week of genuine, helpful content is enough to build a credible presence over time.
05

Not Tracking What's Working

Most businesses spend money on advertising or time on social media with no idea whether it's actually bringing in customers. Without tracking, you're flying blind — spending resources on things that may not be working while missing opportunities that could be.

Google Analytics is free and shows you exactly where your website visitors are coming from, what they look at, and what makes them leave.

The Fix: Install Google Analytics on your website and check it monthly. Even basic data like how many people visit and where they come from will help you make smarter decisions.

Where to Start

If any of these mistakes sound familiar, don't be discouraged — they are extremely common and all fixable. The key is to address them one at a time rather than trying to do everything at once.
